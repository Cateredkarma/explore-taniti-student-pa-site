Add 100+ Pounds To Your Squat!

My favorite exercise, and one that can be very intimidating.

If you are a current or aspiring powerlifter, you no doubt have a goal for squats. However, anyone can have a squat goal. This exercise is a staple for several reasons. A few, although there are many more, include:

  • Lower body muscular development
  • Power development
  • Strength development
  • Core strength

Having a raw squat over 500 pounds (while weighing under 190 pounds), coupled with my deep love and understanding of the involved bio-mechanics, nothing is more fun to me than helping someone improve their squat form.

Habit Formation

The hardest and most important part of fitness is consistency. With dynamic and changing lives, creating new long-term habits can feel difficult.

Don’t worry; we can create a pattern of healthy living that AVOIDS unnecessary stress, anxiety, or feelings of being overwhelmed.

The purpose of exercise is to improve our ability to enjoy life and be HAPPY. If you dread getting up to workout everyday, it will be hard to keep going.

A 1 hour workout is 4% of your day; lets guarantee that 4% is fun and enjoyable.
